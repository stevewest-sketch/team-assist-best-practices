LAYER 2 — THE PROMPT LIBRARY

The exact prompts to type, and what each returns.

Type each one start to finish and fill in the bracketed part. The trick is to be specific — name the scope, the exact dimensions you want, and the length. Team Assist pulls a lot of context, and telling it how to distill is what turns a data dump into something you can use.

Pull broad, but always distill. When you ask for a customer's full history, say what to extract — common contact reasons, what they like and don't like, typical sentiment — and how long — three takeaways, or two to three sentences. Without that, a big pull comes back as a wall of text.
▤

Summarizing

Summarize this customer's history, but only include what's related to [topic].
History filtered to that one topic.
Catch me up on this customer's conversations from the last [30] days.
A window wider than this thread, narrower than the full history.
What has changed with this customer since our last conversation?
A delta from the last contact, not a full re-summary.
What's the customer's most recent message, and is everything earlier in this conversation still relevant to it?
An answer anchored to the latest message.
Summarize this entire conversation in detail. Don't skip or shorten any part of it.
A complete pass, for when a summary came back thin.
Show me only the order history relevant to what the customer is asking about right now.
Order and account history filtered to the live question.

Finding & synthesizing an answer

What's our policy on [specific issue] for [specific situation]?
A short, scoped answer with a citation.
What does our policy say specifically about [detail]?
A narrower answer than a broad policy question returns.
Summarize what we've confirmed about [specific issue] only, in two to three sentences.
A short recap of that issue, not the full history.
Search the knowledge base for everything relevant to what the customer is asking, and give me one answer that covers all of it.
One combined answer when a question has more than one part.

Building context across a session

Summarize everything we've covered in this session so far into what we actually know about the issue.
A synthesis across the whole session, not just the last answer.
Summarize this customer's history as exactly three takeaways I need to know before I respond.
A capped, skimmable version instead of a full writeup.
List the key facts from this conversation as a short bulleted list, no paragraphs.
A format shift, for when a response came back too dense to scan.
Based on this conversation, what information is still missing or unclear before I respond?
A gap check before you draft.

Checking for a pattern

Is this someone who has repeatedly asked for refunds on different orders based on their history?
A precise check against the visible history, not a guess.
Does the customer's history show anything similar to the claim they're making right now?
Whether it matches a prior pattern or stands on its own.

Calls & documentation

A summary of the call so far.
What did the customer say about [topic] earlier in this call?
A specific moment pulled from the transcript.
Summarize this call as a post-contact note — what the customer needed, what was resolved, and any follow-up required.
An internal, factual post-contact note.
List every action I took this conversation — what was done, which record it affected, and the outcome.
An internal action log, final state only.

Order & account

Translation

Translate the customer's most recent message into English.
A translation of what they sent, for you to read.
Translate my reply into [language] before I send it.
A translation of your draft, ready to send.
When two answers come back. If Team Assist returns more than one answer with no way to insert either, don't copy-paste them. Type Draft a reply that covers both of those answers. The draft, and the Use button, appear once you ask for one.
